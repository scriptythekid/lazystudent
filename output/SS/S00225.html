Themenstellung der Lehrveranstaltung

Title: The City, the Child and the Power of Play

Globalization means living with people who differ—racially, ethnically, religiously, or economically—is the most urgent challenge facing civil society today. We tend socially to avoid engaging with people unlike ourselves, and modern politics encourages the politics of the tribe rather than of the city. 

Can architecture do anything about this? The present course looks into the emergence of an urban design tool, what Prof Lefaivre has called the PIP Principle applied to the design of playgrounds as a means of enhancing integration in a multi-cultural neigboorhood, the 15th district of Vienna. 

The child is seen here as the smallest stich in the urban fabric, the locus of least resistance between people. Playgrounds are rethought, as small unis, placed in in-between,  left-over intessticial niches tucked into the neighboorhood and located through participatory processes, following discussions with the users, is children, parents, eachers, local government.

Readings will include Liane Lefaivre,  Mike Davis, Sakia Sassen, Eduard Gleaser, Jane Jacobs, and others
